A Legacy of Service

For over 60 years, the Majlis has been the voice of Ethiopian Muslims, guiding the community through times of change and growth.

The Ethiopian Islamic Affairs Supreme Council was formally established on April 6, 1975 (Rabiul Awal 12, 1395 A.H.), following the massive peaceful protests and struggles of the 1974 revolution. Ethiopian Muslims came together with the intention of unifying and serving the Muslim community.

Throughout its history, the Majlis has adapted to changing times while remaining true to its core mission of promoting Islamic values, education, and social welfare for all Ethiopian Muslims.

Historical Timeline

Key milestones in the journey of the Majlis

1975 - Foundation

The Majlis was formally established on April 6, 1975 (Rabiul Awal 12, 1395 A.H.), unifying Ethiopian Muslims under one representative institution for the first time.

1975-1991 - Derg Era

The Majlis operated under challenging conditions during the Derg regime, maintaining essential religious services despite restrictions.

1991 - Reorganization

Following the fall of the Derg regime, the Majlis was reorganized with expanded mandate and regional presence across Ethiopia.

2000s - Service Expansion

Introduction of new services including formalized Hajj facilitation, halal certification programs, and expanded Islamic education initiatives.

2012-2019 - Reform Movement

Period of significant reform and restructuring to better serve the growing Muslim community and address contemporary challenges.

2020 - Legal Reinstatement

Reinstated as a public entity by the Ethiopian House of Representatives' Decree No. 1207/2020, strengthening its legal foundation.

Present - Digital Transformation

Embracing technology to better serve the community with digital services, online resources, and expanded outreach programs.

Past Presidents

Leaders who shaped the Majlis through the years

Sheikh Mohammed Sani

President (1975-1985)

Founding president who established the organizational structure of the Majlis.

Sheikh Abdul Rahman

President (1991-2005)

Led the reorganization of the Majlis following the political transition of 1991.

Sheikh Elias Redman

President (2005-2012)

The country's highest Islamic authority, representing Ethiopian Muslims both domestically and abroad.
