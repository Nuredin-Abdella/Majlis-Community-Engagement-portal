Who We Are

The Ethiopian Islamic Affairs Supreme Council (Majlis) is the country's highest Islamic authority, a public institution reinstated by decree No. 1207/2020, representing Ethiopian Muslims for the last 60 years both domestically and abroad.

Given the unique relationship our nation, Ethiopia, shares with Islam, the contribution and sacrifice made by fellow Habasha sahabas for the rise and spread of Islam across the world, as well as the rise and fall of our ancestors who underwent countless historical phases.

Following the massive peaceful protests and struggles of the 1974 revolution, Ethiopian Muslims came together to form the Majlis, formally established on April 6, 1975 (Rabiul Awal 12, 1395 A.H.) with the intention of unifying and serving the Muslim community.

Mission & Vision

Guiding principles that drive our commitment to the Muslim community

Our Mission

To create a responsible, organized, conscientious, and competent Muslim community with a higher spiritual, social, and economic life that will actively contribute to the peace and development of the nation in collaboration with all Ethiopians by establishing a modern management system based on ethics and skills in the context of Islamic teachings.

Our Vision

To see a vibrant Ethiopian Muslim community living Islam in accordance with Allah's will, contributing positively to national harmony and development through Islamic values and principles.

Islamic Heritage

EIASC works to safeguard Islamic heritage places so that future generations can enjoy, profit from, and learn from the past.

It also establishes a research institute for doing research and assisting individuals engaged in study to preserve Islamic heritage.
