Understanding Fatwa

A fatwa is a non-binding legal opinion or ruling given by a qualified Islamic scholar on matters pertaining to Islamic law (Sharia).

What is a Fatwa?

A fatwa is an Islamic religious ruling, a scholarly opinion on a matter of Islamic law. It is not binding but provides guidance based on the Quran, Sunnah, and Islamic jurisprudence.

Who Can Issue a Fatwa?

Only qualified Islamic scholars with deep knowledge of the Quran, Hadith, and Islamic jurisprudence are authorized to issue fatwas. The Ethiopian Islamic Affairs Supreme Council has a dedicated Fatwa Committee for this purpose.

Purpose of Fatwa

Fatwas help Muslims understand how to apply Islamic teachings to contemporary issues and personal situations while staying true to the principles of Islam.

Ask a Question

Submit your religious questions to our Fatwa Committee

Please allow 3-5 business days for a response. For urgent matters, please call our Fatwa Hotline.

Guidelines for Asking Questions

  • Be clear and specific in your question
  • Provide relevant context or background information
  • One question per submission
  • Avoid hypothetical or speculative questions
  • Be respectful in your wording
