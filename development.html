Development

Ethiopian Muslims Development Agency (EMDA)

EIASC established EMDA as its development wing to alleviate economic and social problems and to promote development activities. Since its establishment, EMDA has implemented multidimensional, participatory, and need‑based development programs across the country, contributing to maternal and child health in remote pastoralist communities (Afar and Somali), women empowerment, poverty reduction, capacity building, and environmental rejuvenation.

Vision

To see fully self‑reliant, prosperous communities enjoying a decent living standard.

Mission

To address the root causes of poverty and uplift living standards of marginalized and vulnerable communities based on Islamic values and perspectives, including diverse community voices.

Strategic Goals

  • Empower disadvantaged children, OVCs, adolescents, and young girls through quality education.
  • Improve maternal, child, and neonate health and overall wellbeing through FP and SRH programs.
  • Increase households’ access to adequate food and nutrition for a healthy, active life.
  • Reduce vulnerability of orphans and vulnerable children and improve their living conditions.
  • Protect the most needy and broaden sustainable livelihood opportunities.
  • Ensure food security for poor and marginalized communities.
  • Build adaptation and recovery capacities to climate‑induced shocks and stress.
  • Save lives and protect livelihoods during humanitarian crises (drought/conflict).

Organizational Structure (Summary)

EMDA follows EIASC’s structure, governed by a 44‑member General Assembly and a Board of Management providing strategic direction. The Executive Director leads day‑to‑day operations with a management committee, and five directorates (Administration & Finance, Partnerships & Resource Mobilization, Planning/Monitoring & Evaluation, Public Relations & Documentation, and Programs). Programs cover access to basic services, women & family affairs, capacity development, livelihoods & food security, and climate adaptation/DRR/displaced citizens’ rehabilitation.
