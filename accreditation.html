About EIASC Accreditation

The Majlis serves as the official accrediting body for Islamic educational institutions across Ethiopia, ensuring they meet the highest standards of Islamic education and academic excellence.

Our Mission

To ensure that Islamic educational institutions maintain the highest standards of academic quality while staying true to Islamic principles and values.

Our Standards

We evaluate institutions based on comprehensive criteria including curriculum quality, faculty qualifications, facilities, and adherence to Islamic teachings.

Our Reach

Accrediting Islamic educational institutions across Ethiopia, from primary schools to higher education institutions.

Accreditation Process

A step-by-step guide to achieving EIASC accreditation for your institution

1

Initial Inquiry & Application

Submit a letter of intent and complete the preliminary application form. Our team will review your eligibility and provide guidance.

Timeframe: 5-7 business days

2

Self-Study & Documentation

Conduct an institutional self-study using our provided framework and prepare the required documentation.

Timeframe: 2-3 months

3

Document Review

Our accreditation committee will thoroughly review your submission and may request additional information.

Timeframe: 3-4 weeks

4

Site Visit

A team of peer reviewers will conduct an on-site evaluation of your institution's facilities, programs, and operations.

Duration: 2-5 days

5

Evaluation & Decision

The accreditation committee will review the evaluation report and make a final accreditation decision.

Timeframe: 4-6 weeks

6

Accreditation Award

Successful institutions receive an official accreditation certificate valid for three years, along with a detailed evaluation report.

Note: The entire accreditation process typically takes 6-12 months from initial application to final decision, depending on the size and complexity of the institution.

Our accreditation standards cover several key areas including: mission and objectives, governance and administration, educational programs, faculty qualifications, student services, learning resources, and financial stability. A detailed standards manual is provided to all applicant institutions.

Terms and Conditions of Accreditation

1. Accreditation Standards

Institutions must maintain compliance with all EIASC accreditation standards throughout the accreditation period. Any significant changes to the institution's mission, programs, or operations must be reported to EIASC immediately.

2. Validity and Renewal

Accreditation is valid for three years from the date of issuance. Institutions must submit an annual report and apply for re-accreditation at least six months before the expiration date.

3. Site Visits and Evaluations

EIASC reserves the right to conduct announced and unannounced site visits to verify compliance with accreditation standards. Institutions must provide full cooperation and access to all relevant facilities and records.

4. Public Disclosure

Accredited institutions may publicly state their accredited status but must not imply that EIASC accreditation applies to programs or activities not covered by the accreditation.

5. Fee Structure

All fees associated with the accreditation process are non-refundable. Fee schedules are subject to change with 90 days' notice to accredited institutions.

6. Termination of Accreditation

EIASC reserves the right to revoke accreditation if an institution fails to maintain the required standards, provides false information, or engages in practices contrary to Islamic principles or Ethiopian laws.

7. Appeals Process

Institutions have the right to appeal accreditation decisions following the formal appeals process outlined in the EIASC Accreditation Manual.
