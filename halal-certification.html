About Halal Certification

The Ethiopian Islamic Affairs Supreme Council (EIASC) is the official body authorized to issue Halal certification in Ethiopia, ensuring that products and services comply with Islamic Shariah law.

What is Halal Certification?

Halal certification verifies that products and services meet the requirements of Islamic law and are permissible for consumption and use by Muslims. It covers food, beverages, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and other consumer goods.

Why Get Certified?

Halal certification opens doors to domestic and international Muslim markets, enhances consumer confidence, and demonstrates your commitment to quality and ethical business practices.

Global Recognition

Our certification is recognized by major Halal certification bodies worldwide, facilitating international trade and export opportunities for Ethiopian businesses.

Certification Process

Follow these steps to obtain your Halal certification

1

Application Submission

Complete and submit the Halal certification application form along with required documents.

2

Document Review

Our team reviews your application and supporting documents for completeness and compliance.

3

Inspection & Audit

Our certified inspectors conduct an on-site audit of your facilities and processes.

4

Laboratory Testing

Products may be tested in accredited laboratories to verify Halal status.

5

Certification Decision

Based on the audit findings, a decision is made regarding certification.

6

Certificate Issuance

Upon approval, the Halal certificate is issued and your business is listed in our directory.

Note: The entire process typically takes 15-30 working days, depending on the complexity of your operations and the completeness of your application.

Terms and Conditions

1. Certification Process

The certification process includes application review, facility inspection, and product evaluation. EIASC reserves the right to approve or reject any application based on compliance with Halal requirements.

2. Validity and Renewal

Certificates are valid for one year from the date of issue and must be renewed annually. EIASC will notify you 60 days before expiration.

3. Inspection Rights

EIASC reserves the right to conduct unannounced inspections of certified facilities to ensure ongoing compliance with Halal standards.

4. Fees and Payments

All fees are non-refundable and must be paid in full before certification is granted. Additional fees may apply for special circumstances or additional services.

5. Use of Certification Mark

The Halal certification mark may only be used on products and marketing materials that have been certified. Misuse of the mark may result in immediate revocation of certification.

6. Compliance

Certified businesses must maintain compliance with all Halal requirements and notify EIASC of any changes to products, ingredients, or processes that may affect certification.

7. Termination

EIASC reserves the right to suspend or revoke certification if a business fails to maintain compliance with certification standards or terms and conditions.
