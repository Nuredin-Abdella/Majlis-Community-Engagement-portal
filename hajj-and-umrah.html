Hajj: The Fifth Pillar of Islam

Hajj is a mandatory religious duty for Muslims that must be carried out at least once in their lifetime by all adult Muslims who are physically and financially capable of undertaking the journey, and can support their family during their absence.

Key Rituals of Hajj:

Ihram

Entering the state of consecration by wearing special white garments

Wuquf at Arafat

Standing in prayer at the plain of Arafat on the 9th of Dhul-Hijjah

Tawaf

Circumambulation of the Kaaba seven times in a counter-clockwise direction

Sa'i

Walking seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwah

    Pilgrimage Requirements

    Essential information and documents needed for your journey

    Hajj Pilgrimage Requirements

    • Must be a Muslim
    • Must be of sound mind and physically capable
    • Must be an adult (have reached puberty)
    • Must have the financial means to support the journey and dependents left behind
    • Must have a valid passport with at least 6 months validity
    • Must complete all required vaccinations

    Umrah Pilgrimage Requirements

    • Must be a Muslim
    • Must have a valid Umrah visa
    • Passport with at least 6 months validity
    • Completed vaccination requirements
    • Confirmed travel and accommodation arrangements

    Required Documents

    • Original and copy of valid passport
    • Passport-size photographs with white background
    • Vaccination certificates (COVID-19, Meningitis, etc.)
    • Proof of relationship (for family members traveling together)
    • Marriage certificate (for couples traveling together)
    • Letter of no objection from employer (if employed)

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Find answers to common questions about Hajj and Umrah

    Hajj is the annual Islamic pilgrimage to Mecca that is a mandatory religious duty for Muslims that must be carried out at least once in their lifetime by all adult Muslims who are physically and financially capable. Umrah is a pilgrimage to Mecca that can be undertaken at any time of the year and is considered a lesser pilgrimage.

    The cost of Hajj packages varies depending on the type of accommodation, transportation, and services included. Please contact your nearest registration center for the most up-to-date pricing information.

    All pilgrims must be vaccinated against COVID-19, meningitis (ACWY), and seasonal influenza. Additional vaccinations may be required based on your country of origin and current health regulations. Please check with your local health authorities for the most current requirements.

    You can check your application status by visiting the registration center where you submitted your application or by contacting the Ethiopian Islamic Affairs Supreme Council's Hajj and Umrah department at +251 114 664 941.

    Have more questions? Contact our support team for assistance.
