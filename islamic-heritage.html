Islamic Heritage of Ethiopia

Preserving the rich Islamic legacy that has shaped Ethiopia's history and culture for over 1,400 years

Our Islamic Legacy

Ethiopia holds a unique place in Islamic history, being the land that provided refuge to the first Muslims during the time of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Our heritage is a testament to the deep roots of Islam in this ancient land.

615 CE

First Hijrah to Abyssinia

The first group of Muslims, including Prophet Muhammad's daughter Ruqayyah and her husband Uthman ibn Affan, migrated to Abyssinia (modern-day Ethiopia) seeking protection from persecution in Mecca.

7th Century

Establishment of First Mosques

Al-Nejashi Mosque, one of the oldest mosques in Africa, was built in the 7th century in Negash, Tigray, marking the beginning of Islamic architecture in the region.

15th Century

Spread of Islam in the Horn

Islam spread through trade routes and the establishment of sultanates, leading to the construction of numerous mosques and Islamic learning centers across the region.
