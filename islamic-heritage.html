Preserving Our Legacy

Ethiopia has a rich Islamic heritage dating back to the earliest days of Islam, when the first Muslims sought refuge in the land of Habasha.

EIASC works to safeguard Islamic heritage places so that future generations can enjoy, profit from, and learn from the past. Our heritage preservation program includes documentation, restoration, and educational initiatives.

We also establish research institutes for conducting research and assisting individuals engaged in study to preserve Islamic heritage and promote understanding of Ethiopia's unique place in Islamic history.

Historic Sites

Discover Ethiopia's most significant Islamic heritage sites

Najashi Mosque

One of the oldest mosques in Africa, built during the time of the first Hijra when early Muslims sought refuge with King Najashi of Abyssinia.

Negash, Tigray Region

Harar Jugol

The ancient walled city of Harar, known as the fourth holiest city in Islam, featuring 82 mosques and 102 shrines within its walls.

Harar, Harari Region

Sheikh Hussein Shrine

A major pilgrimage site in the Bale Zone, attracting thousands of pilgrims annually to honor the 12th-century Islamic scholar.

Bale Zone, Oromia Region

Anwar Mosque

The largest mosque in Ethiopia, located in Addis Ababa, serving as a central place of worship for the capital's Muslim community.

Addis Ababa
