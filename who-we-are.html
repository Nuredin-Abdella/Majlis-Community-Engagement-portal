Our Story

The Majlis stands as a beacon of unity and guidance for Ethiopian Muslims, fostering religious harmony and community development since its establishment.

Formally established on April 6, 1975 (Rabiul Awal 12, 1395 A.H.), the Majlis was born from the collective aspirations of Ethiopian Muslims to have a unified voice and institution to serve their religious and social needs.

Over the decades, the Majlis has evolved to meet the changing needs of the Muslim community while staying true to its core mission of promoting Islamic values, education, and social welfare.

Our Mission

To guide and serve the Ethiopian Muslim community by promoting Islamic teachings, fostering unity, and addressing the religious and social needs of Muslims while maintaining peaceful coexistence with all faiths.

Our Vision

To be a model Islamic institution that ensures the spiritual and social well-being of Ethiopian Muslims, contributing to national harmony and development through Islamic values and principles.

Our Journey

Key milestones in the history of the Majlis

1975

Establishment of the Majlis on April 6, 1975.

1991

Reorganization following the fall of the Derg regime, expanding community services.

2020

Reinstated as a public entity by the Ethiopian House of Representatives' Decree No. 1207/2020.
